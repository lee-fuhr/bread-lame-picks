A good bread lame turns a loaf of dough into a work of art, letting you control how the crust rips open in the oven. The right one should feel natural in your hand, hold the blade securely, and let you adjust depth and angle without thinking. There are two main styles: those with a fixed handle and replaceable blade (traditional), and those with an adjustable depth guard or a curved ergonomic grip. Your choice depends on how much control you want and whether you prefer a lightweight tool for quick slashes or a heavier one for more deliberate cuts.
