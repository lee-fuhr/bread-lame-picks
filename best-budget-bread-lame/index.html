A bread lame is the single biggest upgrade you can make to your homemade loaves. Without a proper scoring tool, dough tends to tear and bake into a shapeless blob, but a thin, sharp blade lets you control where the loaf expands, creating those iconic ear cracks and an even, open crumb. The challenge is finding a lame that stays sharp out of the box and doesn’t cost more than a few bags of flour. When shopping for a budget lame, focus on three things: blade-holder style (fixed vs. replaceable), ergonomics (how the handle feels in your hand), and included blades. Many cheap lames skimp on both blade quality and quantity, leaving you with a dull blade after a few uses.
